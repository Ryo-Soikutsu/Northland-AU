# Overview
Abyssal-derived materials (also known as the abyssal material lineage, or colloquially as "abyssium") are a class of substances originating from crude abyssal plasma, an unstable and high-energy feedstock substance. The term covers both the raw input materials, as well as the final products after refinement and processing. It is sometimes also incorrectly referred to as "abyssal-group elements or metals", typically in casual speech with non-experts. 

Abyssal-derived materials are typically specialized in nature, but there is a general-use abyssal plasma which is produced at various steps in the processing chain (called "abyssal refining"). Abyssal refining is an energy-intensive process, and is currently energy-negative as of current developments. The final products of abyssal refining are used in almost all aspects of Foundation operations, including inter-dimensional travel, advanced computer and network systems and weapons. 
# Properties
Crude abyssal plasma is very unstable and highly energetic, as well as possessing an "entropic" (i.e exponentially corrosive) effect. In its crude state, abyssal plasma is not safe or effective to use, and must be refined. Crude abyssal plasma can be held in stasis chambers to minimize surface contact with objects, to prevent the corrosive effects from being applied.

Abyssal-derived materials possess a wide range of unique properties, which makes them more suited for certain roles. For example, precision-grade abyssal plasma can be "programmed" with specific attributes, making them ideal for manufacturing advanced abyssal supercomputers.
