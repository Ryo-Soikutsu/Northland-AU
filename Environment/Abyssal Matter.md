# Overview
Abyssal-derived materials (also known as the abyssal material lineage, or colloquially as "abyssium") are a class of substances originating from crude abyssal plasma, an unstable and high-energy feedstock substance. The term covers both the raw input materials, as well as the final products after refinement and processing. It is sometimes also incorrectly referred to as "abyssal-group elements or metals", typically in casual speech with non-experts. 

Abyssal-derived materials are typically specialized in nature, but there is a general-use abyssal plasma which is produced at various steps in the processing chain (called "abyssal refining"). Abyssal refining is an energy-intensive process, and currently consumes more power than it can produce through its byproducts. The refined outputs of abyssal refining are used in almost all aspects of Foundation operations, including inter-dimensional travel, advanced computer and network systems and weapons. 
# Properties
Crude abyssal plasma is very unstable and highly energetic, as well as possessing an "entropic" (i.e exponentially corrosive) effect. In its crude state, abyssal plasma is neither safe nor effective to use, and must be refined. Crude abyssal plasma can be held in stasis chambers to minimize surface contact with objects, to prevent any corrosive effects from being applied.

Abyssal-derived materials possess a wide range of unique properties, which makes them more suited for certain roles. For example, precision-grade abyssal plasma can be "programmed" with specific attributes, making them ideal for manufacturing advanced abyssal supercomputers. A list of the products and their unique properties is listed below:
- Precision-grade: 
	- Exhibits "programmable" traits at the particle level
	- Can be altered to possess properties and traits beyond conventional physical constraints
- Weapons-grade: 
	- High energy release rate and controllable instability
	- Can rapidly transition into an energetic state under specific triggers
- High-grade: 
	- High stability and fault tolerance
	- Exhibits incredible levels of structural integrity and little to no degradation under extreme conditions
	- Also exhibits a low level of self-restoration, insignificant to long-term use
- Fuel-grade: 
	- High energy density with easily controlled reaction dynamics
	- Residual fusion byproducts can hold large amounts of energy relative to the amount of material required
- General use: 
	- Versatile baseline material
	- Can also be used for transmutation into other elements
- Abyssal slag: 
	- Effectively useless byproduct
	- Can be ejected into abyssal space as seed material for abyssal plasma orefields
