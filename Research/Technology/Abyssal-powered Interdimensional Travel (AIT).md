# Description
(Note: Requires prerequisite knowledge of the [[Abyssal Dimension]])
Abyssal-powered Interdimensional Travel (AIT) technology comprises of a set of innovations that takes advantage of the unique properties of the Abyssal dimension, and uses them to enable rapid travel between universes and/or dimensions. All Foundation-operated space-faring vessels that travel within the abyssal dimension, or between universes are required to have warp and jump drives rated for AIT usage installed. 

# Contents
The AIT umbrella term includes the following technologies:
- Abyssal Gate Array module
- Abyssal-infused General Warp Core
- Abyssal-infused Military Warp Core
- Localized Stable Field Generator
## Abyssal Gate Array Module
The Abyssal Gate Array (AGA) is a special module that allows the creation and destruction of tunnels between abyssal space and normal space. AGAs are the only way for ships to travel between the two spaces, and as such is mandatory for all fleets to have at least one AGA-equipped vessel active at all times. 

Due to the immense power and space consumption of the AGA module, only capital-class vessels or larger can be equipped with this module. For smaller ships, such as research vessels and escorts, a dedicated Aegis-class fleet support carrier must be flown alongside the main fleet, fitted with the module. As abyssal tunnels are very unstable, it is forbidden to idle in the tunnels, or deviate from the calculated path, lest the tunnels collapse and leave the travelers stranded between dimensions. 

## Abyssal-infused Warp Core Stabilizer (AWCS)
A modified version of the basic warp core stabilizer, designed to enable FTL travel within abyssal space, as well as enhance the warp shielding generated to resist the corrosive effects of abyssal matter. This modular upgrade can be inserted into most warp and jump drives of class 3 or larger (cruiser-sized ships or larger). AWCS are more expensive to produce compared to standard warp core stabilizers, due to the added shielding required and use of exotic abyssal metals. Fuel and power consumptions are increased slightly when using AWCS, but is not significant enough to warrant an upgrade to the ship's engineering and power generation. 

## Localized Stable Field Generator
While not required for direct abyssal travel, the Localized Stable Field Generator (LSFG) is mandatory when visiting dimensions that have certain environmental restrictions. For example, low-tech worlds or worlds that possess a high level of magical influence. LSFGs require exotic abyssal fuels to run, which are expensive to produce in larger quantities. As such, ships using LSFGs are only permitted to run them for a maximum of 15-20 minutes at a time, either to insert or extract personnel, equipment and research data from outposts and mission areas. 

When activated, the LSFG creates a large field around the ship activating the module, and modifies it to include metaphysical or environmental properties that will allow normal operations of the ship's technology and systems. During the activation duration, any ships within the area of effect will also be affected by the new properties, and will not retain any of the effects when they leave the area. Due to the relative instability of the field, it is not advised to move the ship after the module has been activated.
